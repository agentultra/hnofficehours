Github, freenode #hnofficehours (once we start working)

Background

A few weeks ago, kentf posted on Hacker News and offered to act as a sounding board for any problem fellow HNers might have. We think that's awesome and want to build a site to those facilitate interactions.

The details of the execution are very much up in the air and we'd like to ask for suggestions/recommendations/help on the project.

Proposal

Everything is up for discussion, but to get this started, we've described what we think is the Min Viable Product:

  • Display list of people. Clicking a person shows profile and availability.
  • Display available office hours as a list of datetimes. Clicking a time shows who is available at that time.
  • Vanilla django auth + hn profile validation *
  • “Easy” to create office hours. i should be able to say “every wednesday for the following 4 weeks” and similar. As limited by django-calendar.
  • Link your office hours to a google calendar rss feed
  • One-click to Skype

Additional thoughts:

  • should non HN users be allowed to create accounts? we can restrict this by keeping django account is_active = False until hn profile validation occurs. can non-hn users (non authenticated users) use the site? can they view office hours? my vote is yes. would some office hours holders like to restrict their office hours to be only open for fellow authenticated HNers?
  • could we do one-click to gchat, too?
  • twilio integration. "I want to be available for phone calls, but don't want to publicly list my phone number." can we obscure a number with twilio and have it just forward to the correct number?
  • support office hours requests? "I'm having problems with X. contact me: null@example.com"
  • search by area of expertise. "I want to find people who are holding office hours that know about SEO"

HN hackathon

We're Pybrew and we work on Django web apps. This weekend we want to build a Minimum Viable Product for HN Office Hours with a group of people from Hacker News. We'll get a lot further and build something better as a collaborative effort.

We're inviting anyone who is interested in contributing to this effort in any capacity to reach out to us.

We're interested in approaching the project this way because it'll be more fun, we want to meet other hackers, and we think all parties involved will be able to learn from it. Any potential users of such a site are especially encouraged to contribute — help mold this project in a way that you would enjoy using it.

Anyone who wants to contribute can. There is no contribution too small.

The project will be hosted on Github. We just started posting notes in the Wiki. For now that seems like a good place to store comments and notes. Additionally, we'll start creating Issues (Tickets) based on any feedback we get from potential users and collaborators.
